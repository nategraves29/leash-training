LEASH TRAINING GUIDE

Lead Engagement and Support Hub

INTRODUCTION

This training guide will teach you how to effectively work in the LEASH role at Scenthound. LEASH is the frontline connection between potential customers and our Scenters, managing leads, booking trials, and ensuring every customer receives excellent service from their very first interaction.

LEAD MANAGEMENT WORKFLOW

Daily Start Routine

Step 1: Check SMS Magic (All Scenters)
Review all three Scenters' SMS Magic for responses to:
‚Ä¢ Marketing texts
‚Ä¢ Personal outreach texts either you or another team member have sent
Note: If someone responds to a text but there's no outgoing message from you in the thread, they're responding to an automated marketing text.
Step 2: Call New Leads (Priority #1)
New Leads are hot leads and must be contacted as quickly as possible throughout the day.

New Lead Standards:

  • Contact within 5 minutes of lead generation (ideal)
  • New Leads take priority over all other outbound activities
  • Leads generate overnight, so process morning leads first thing
Step 3: Active Trial Leads
Begin contacting the Active Trial Leads for all three scenters.

OUTBOUND CALL PROTOCOL

Contact Sequence

Day 1 - New Lead
  1. Call the lead
  2. If no answer: Leave voicemail
  3. Send SMS (unless FOSA shows they received an automated marketing text that same day)
  4. If they received a text the previous day, send a personal text to differentiate from automated messages
  5. Mark as Called - Left Voicemail with comment "[Initials] - left VM and sent SMS" in FOSA
Day 2 - Active Trial Lead
  1. Call again
  2. If no answer: Do NOT leave voicemail
  3. Mark as Called - Connected with comment "[Initials] - 2nd contact no VM" in FOSA
Day 3

Send a text

It‚Äôs good practice to create multiple message variations with different tones so we aren‚Äôt sending the same text repeatedly to our leads.

Mark as Texted with comment "[Initials] - sent SMS" in FOSA

Day 4

Call and leave another voicemail

Again, vary the tone and content in your voicemails so we don‚Äôt sound scripted or lacking in care and conviction.

Mark as Called - Left Voicemail with comment
"[Initials]" in FOSA

Days 5+
  • Alternate between texting and voicemail by day with correct marks and comments in FOSA
  • Around Day 5-6: If no response, pause outreach for a couple days
Important Philosophies: We are persistent, not spammers. Use your judgment. If outreach feels too heavy, pause temporarily.

TRIAL BOOKING PROCESS

Required Information Checklist

When a lead converts and wants to book a trial appointment, collect the following information to complete their MyTime profile:

You should gather this information, whether over the phone or by text, and update their MyTime profile in real time as you‚Äôre speaking with them.

Pet Parent Information:

  • First name
  • Last name
  • Phone number
  • Email address

Dog Information:

  • Dog's name
  • Breed
  • Weight
  • Date of birth (or approximate)
  • Gender
  • Spay/neuter status
  • Veterinarian name
Critical Health Documentation:
‚ñ° Is the dog up to date on rabies vaccine?
‚ñ° Does the pet parent have a physical or digital copy of the rabies certificate?
If they have the document: Text them the correct Scenter email after the call so they can send it over. If they only have a hard copy, ask them to bring it to their appointment.

If they don't have the document: Call their veterinarian and request that the rabies certificate be emailed to the appropriate Scenter address. Note that if the vaccine was done through Vetco, the pet parent must obtain the document themselves, as Vetco only releases records directly to them.

Gathering Intelligence for Service Quality

The following information directly impacts the quality of service and trial conversion rates. Take detailed notes.

Temperament & Mobility:
Ask about any behavioral or physical considerations the team should be aware of for the appointment:
  • Anxiety or nervousness
  • Reactivity to other dogs
  • Mobility issues or injuries
  • Sensitivity (paws, ears, etc.)
Coat Condition Assessment:

Short-haired dogs:
  • Ask about dandruff or skin issues (opportunity to recommend additional services)
Medium and long-haired dogs:
  • Critical: Ask about mats or tangles
  • Explain that getting mats wet can worsen them
  • Obtain consent to trim out mats if necessary
  • Sales opportunity: Recommend brush out and blow dry service for dogs prone to matting
Pet Parent Expectations:
Understand what they're hoping to achieve from the trial and ongoing services. This helps the team tailor their approach.

TRIAL CONVERSION FORMS

Non-Negotiable Rule:
Every trial booking requires a completed Trial Conversion form in Kintone. No exceptions.

Form Purpose

The Trial Conversion form equips the in-person team with the intelligence needed to:

  • Deliver higher quality service
  • Address specific pet and pet parent needs
  • Increase same-day trial conversions
  • Target pain points in their sales pitch

Key Form Fields

Top Fields:
Link to MyTime Profile, Location of Trial Appointment, Date of Trial Appointment, Time of Trial Appointment, Dog Name(s), Pet Parent Name(s).
Services Booked:
Check the appropriate boxes for the services that are booked in the trial appointment.
Pain Points (Selectable Menu):
Select all applicable pain points to alert the team:
  • Kennel anxiety
  • Dog reactivity
  • Paw sensitivity
  • Injury
  • Elderly
  • Rescue
  • Other relevant concerns
Coat Condition/General Upkeep:
For short-hair dogs this can be a simple "short hair/regular shed." For medium or long-hair dogs give a more in-depth response such as "brushes at home, shouldn't have any mats or tangles." or "PP said they were pretty bad about brushing, may have mats behind ears. PP said OK to clip mats as needed."
Description of Conversation:
Include all relevant information gathered during the booking call that will help the team succeed. This is your opportunity to paint a complete picture.

Critical Step: After completing the Kintone form, copy the Description of Conversation and paste it into the client's MyTime profile. Pin this note so it's visible to all team members during the appointment.
Sales Pitch Ideas:
Based on your conversation, suggest specific approaches the team should take. What information should they emphasize? What concerns should they address?
The Bottom Line:
The more detailed and thoughtful your Trial Conversion form, the better equipped the team is to convert that trial into a member. This form is the bridge between LEASH and the in-person team.
PP = Pet Parent
